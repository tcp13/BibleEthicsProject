The Bible Ethics Project


Enter a controversial topic in the search bar to begin!





Or start by clicking on one of these examples!





Welcome to the Bible Ethics Project, created by Tyler Puckett for his Honors Bible assignment. Simply enter a controversial topic into the search bar to the left, and use online resources to view your topic through a Godly worldview.


This website was created from scratch using the programming languages HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with the jQuery library and Bootstrap framework. Click here to see the over 700 lines of source files and custom scripts I wrote for this site.

"All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the servant of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work."

(2 Timothy 3:16-17)


When researching the ethics of a topic, the best place to start is the Bible - God's source of absolute truth. The links to the right will automatically search online Bible resources for specific mentions of "your topic". Make sure to read the surrounding verses of your results, so that they are not taken out of context!

Bible Gateway searches the NIV Bible for specific mentions of your topic.


ESV Online searches the ESV Study Bible for specific mentions of your topic along with footnotes, historical context, and other useful information to help you understand Scripture.


Bible Study Tools searches a database with hundreds of versions and translations for any mention of your topic.



"For the word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soul and of spirit, of joints and of marrow, and discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart."

(Hebrews 4:12)


After finding literal mentions of your topic, topical Bibles and other reference sources can be used to find more general Biblical principles that might apply.

Open Bible is a dynamic topical Bible that finds generalized verses and references about your topic.


Bible Study Tools uses a database to search for articles and Biblical passages related to your topic.



"Do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewal of your mind, that by testing you may discern what is the will of God, what is good and acceptable and perfect."

(Romans 12:2)


After studying your topic in the Bible, take a moment to think about other Christians. To the right are some specific questions to consider.

  • Would your topic tempt other Christians to sin?

  • Would your topic be offensive to other Christians?

  • Would your topic hinder someone's relationship with Christ?
